The Manifesto

A Platform Built on Evidence,
Not Promises

This manifesto is grounded in two years of lived experience inside Maseno's nursing programme — organised into three transformative pillars, each backed by concrete, measurable actions.

W
Pillar One

Student Welfare

  • Establish a dedicated Student Welfare Office with a trained counsellor available minimum three days per week.
  • Create an anonymous digital grievance portal with trackable case resolution timelines.
  • Introduce peer-support networks and a financial hardship emergency fund with defined application processes.
  • Negotiate improved housing conditions and transport allowances for clinical rotation periods.
  • Enforce a zero-tolerance policy on academic harassment with an independent student appeals committee.
A
Pillar Two

Academic Excellence

  • Reform the clinical posting process ensuring equitable, timely, and specialty-relevant rotations for all students.
  • Establish regular student–faculty dialogue forums (minimum once per term) with published outcomes.
  • Introduce a structured mentorship programme pairing Year 1 students with senior students and graduate professionals.
  • Lobby for updated curriculum content integrating WHO standards alongside Kenya's clinical training requirements.
  • Create a student-led research programme to build academic confidence and professional identity.
C
Pillar Three

Clinical Innovation

  • Full institutional deployment of ClinPlacer within six months of taking office — replacing paper and spreadsheets entirely.
  • Introduce a health informatics elective covering EHR systems, telemedicine, and Kenya's KHSSP digital mandate.
  • Partner with regional hospitals to create simulation-based training labs for high-stakes clinical procedures.
  • Establish a student innovation fund for healthcare technology pilot projects developed within Maseno.
  • Coordinate clinical exchange programmes with East African nursing schools to broaden exposure.
